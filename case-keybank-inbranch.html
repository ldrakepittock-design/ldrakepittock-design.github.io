Context

The opportunity

KeyBank's retail branches were struggling with inconsistent client experiences. The quality of interactions between bankers and clients varied widely — and leadership knew that the banker-client relationship was a key differentiator in a competitive market.

The challenge wasn't technology or products. It was the conversation itself. As Project Lead and Service Designer, I was asked to scope and deliver a solution within 4 months that could realistically be implemented across the full branch network.

I started by mapping the current state through service blueprinting and a cross-functional stakeholder workshop — bringing together teams from Sales Enablement, L&D, Corporate Communications, and Marketing to build a shared picture of the problem before anyone started designing solutions.

Research

Learning from the people already doing it well

Rather than designing from scratch, I recruited and led 14 in-depth interviews with bankers already known for creating exceptional client experiences — selected across roles, geographies, and tenure levels, from 9-month tellers to 24-year private client bankers across 6 states.

The research question wasn't "what should bankers do differently?" It was "what are the best bankers already doing that others aren't?" That reframe changed everything about what we found.

The most successful bankers don't think about selling — they think about helping. They rely on their team's expertise, not just their own. And they think about the client relationship in and out of the branch, not just within it.

Using narrative analysis, I distilled the interviews into 5 core mindsets that separated high performers from average ones — not behaviors to prescribe, but ways of thinking that produced consistently better interactions.

Successful teammates think about… Rather than…
Helping their clients Selling to clients
Relying on their team's expertise Relying on just their own expertise
The interaction in and out of the branch The interaction just in the branch
Creating unique experiences Following just expected norms
Discovering needs Fulfilling wants
Process

From insight to model

The 5 mindsets became the foundation for design. In two major cross-functional workshops, I facilitated ideation with stakeholders across Sales Enablement, L&D, Corporate Communications, and Marketing — generating 20+ versions of a conversational model, pressure-testing 3 candidates with the group, and refining the final version into something a banker with any level of experience could internalize and apply.

Design

The Relationship Experience Model

The final model — built around 5 C's: Care, Connect, Clarify, Carry Out, and Conclude and Continue — gave bankers a shared language and a concrete structure for every client interaction. Care sits at the center: not as a step in a process, but as the underlying orientation that shapes every moment of the conversation.

The model wasn't designed to be a script. It was designed to be internalized — making explicit what the best bankers were already doing intuitively, in a form that could be taught, practiced, and measured.

Reflection

What I'd do differently

The biggest gap in our research was not seeing the work in context. Budget constraints kept me from conducting in-branch observation, which meant our insights came entirely from what bankers told us — not what we watched them do. Behavioral research in the actual environment would have surfaced edge cases and workarounds we couldn't have anticipated from interviews alone. If I ran this project again, in-branch observation would be non-negotiable.
