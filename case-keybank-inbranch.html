Service Design Research Banking · KeyBank

How might we improve in-branch experiences?

A bank wanted to fundamentally shift how bankers interact with clients. I led a 6-month end-to-end project — from service blueprinting and ethnographic research to piloting and rolling out a new conversational model across 900+ branches.

Context

The opportunity

KeyBank's retail branches were struggling with inconsistent client experiences. The quality of interactions between bankers and clients varied widely — and leadership knew that the relationship between banker and client was a key differentiator in a competitive market.

As Project Lead and Service Designer, I was asked to scope and deliver a solution within 6 months that could realistically be implemented across the full branch network.

Role: Project Lead · Service Designer
Process

A full design cycle in six months

01 Scope & Current State Service blueprints + stakeholder workshop to map current state and surface opportunity areas
02 Research 14 interviews with top-performing bankers across role, location, and tenure
03 Synthesis Narrative analysis revealed 5 core mindsets shared by the most successful teammates
04 Ideation 20+ versions of a conversational model, workshopped down to 3 candidates with stakeholders
05 Pilot & Iterate Tested in a Dayton, OH pilot branch, measuring efficacy before network rollout
06 Implement Launched across 900+ branches with full training curriculum
Research

Learning from the people already doing it well

Rather than designing from scratch, I recruited and led 14 in-depth interviews with bankers who were already known for creating exceptional client experiences — selected across roles, geographies, and tenure levels.

Using narrative analysis, I surfaced 5 core mindsets that separated high performers from average ones.

The most successful bankers think about helping clients rather than selling to clients. They rely on their team's expertise — not just their own. And they think about the interaction in and out of the branch, not just within it.

Design

The Relationship Experience Model

I ideated over 20 versions of a conversational model, workshopped 3 candidates with cross-functional stakeholders (Sales Enablement, L&D, Corporate Communications), and refined the final model into a teachable framework.

The final model — built around the mnemonic CARE: Clarify → Connect → Carry Out → Conclude & Continue — gave bankers a shared language and a concrete structure for every client interaction.

Reflection

What I'd do differently

The pilot approach was invaluable — it gave us real-world validation before committing to a network-wide rollout. If I were to run this project again, I'd build in a more structured feedback loop from frontline bankers during the pilot phase, rather than primarily measuring outcomes. Bankers had nuanced feedback about edge cases that we discovered too late to fully incorporate into the initial training materials.

I'd also push for a longer pilot window — 2 months was enough to see early signals, but behavioral change in service delivery is gradual. A 4-month pilot would have given us more confidence in sustainability.
