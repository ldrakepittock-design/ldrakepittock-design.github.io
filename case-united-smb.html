B2B Research Concept Design Aviation · United Airlines

How might we win with small businesses?

United Airlines wanted to better engage small business customers — a largely underserved segment. Through deep ethnographic research with 20 CEOs and CFOs, I uncovered a core insight that reframed how the airline should think about this audience, leading to a new loyalty program concept.

Context

A largely ignored segment with enormous potential

Small businesses represent a significant portion of business travel, yet most airline loyalty programs were designed for either individual frequent flyers or large corporations. United wanted to understand whether a dedicated small business offering could unlock this underserved market.

As Project Lead, I scoped and ran the full research process — from secondary market sizing to ethnographic fieldwork to a concept prototype with financial modeling.

Role: Project Lead
Process

Research-first, prototype to validate

01 Secondary Research Market sizing to identify the size of prize in the SMB segment
02 Recruitment Recruited CEOs and CFOs from 10 diverse small businesses
03 Field Research 3-hour in-context ethnographic interviews at each business's office
04 Insights Session 1-day video-based session immersing stakeholders from IT, Sales, Product & Marketing
05 Ideation Evaluated solutions across desirability, viability, and feasibility
06 Prototype Concept deliverable: P&L, member journey, product roadmap, growth trajectory
Research

The practices insight: how SMBs are different

The 3-hour in-office ethnographic interviews revealed something fundamental that changed how we framed the entire opportunity. Small businesses don't operate like large corporations (governed by explicit policies), nor like individuals (governed by personal preferences).

Small businesses abide by "practices" — implicit customs grown by culture, flexible but ambiguous. Where a corporate traveler says "I have to," an SMB owner says "I should." This distinction has profound implications for how to design a loyalty program for them.

This reframe meant that a successful program had to work with the informal, peer-influenced nature of SMB decision-making — not against it. Peer referrals, trust networks, and community belonging would matter far more than a standard points system.

Insights Session

Bringing stakeholders into the field — without leaving the room

One of the most powerful moments of this project was the 1-day video-based insights session. Rather than presenting findings in a deck, I curated video clips from the ethnographic interviews and structured the day so that stakeholders from IT, Sales, Product, and Marketing could experience the research themselves.

After watching participants talk about their travel habits, peer networks, and decision-making processes, stakeholders ideated solutions grounded in real customer context — not assumptions. The quality of ideas was markedly different than in sessions that don't use this approach.

Concept

An invite-only program built on peer trust

The final concept was an invite-only small business loyalty program where individual travelers could choose travel benefits tailored to their business needs — not a one-size-fits-all corporate account.

Key design principles drawn from the research: members refer peers (not the airline), packages flex around business type, and benefits go beyond flights to reduce business friction. The concept deliverable included a member journey, product roadmap, initial P&L, and 5-year growth trajectory.

Reflection

What I'd do differently

The video-based insights session was the most impactful facilitation format I've used — but it requires significant prep time to select and edit the right clips. In future projects, I'd build this editing time into the project plan from the start, rather than treating it as an add-on after analysis is complete.

I'd also have pushed to test the concept with actual SMB owners before finalizing the P&L projections. The financial model was built on assumptions that would have benefited from even a lightweight validation round with potential program members.
